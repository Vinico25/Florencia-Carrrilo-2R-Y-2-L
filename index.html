Happy birthday, Florencia.

I will always choose you.

I'm sorry I'm not writing this on paper this time around, but I wanted to try something different. I know you usually switch to English when you want to talk about more emotionally intense things. You know it's not exactly my strong suit and that I practically hate it, but I've actually come to like it thanks to your Spanglish. There might be some weird or awkward words here, but I really tried my best with my humble English dictionary, without translators or anything like that. I wanted to do this because with you I can be truly authentic, and that's something I usually struggle with a lot. I'm writing this to you today precisely because it's your birthday. And when I read the letter you wrote to me back then, I realized we have a disagreement about how all this started. You insist it began with that question, but the truth is I was just playing dumb out of sheer shyness and because of a misunderstanding with your answer. For me, that question simply doesn't exist; it was never a deciding factor. I remember perfectly well that I told you about my dogs and Superman, and that's not something I do with just anyone. From that moment on, I've really gotten to know you. I know that a lot of the time you can be unpleasant, unfriendly, annoying, angry, bossy, obsessive, hysterical, neurotic, dramatic, and a thousand other things. But the truth is, I don't care. I've always believed that the people you love most aren't loved for their good qualities, but in spite of all their bad ones; because, in the end, they aren't even bad in the eyes of the right person, because honestly, I wouldn't want you to stop being any of those things because they're a big part of who you are. And I know very well that I have my own problems too. I carry my own demons, my indecision, my negativity, and often I'm not the best company. I'm fully aware of that, and that's why I appreciate so much that you've decided to stay even when things get tough, because I know that sometimes I'm the one who complicates them. But you've always seen the best in me, even in those moments when I see absolutely nothing good. In all this time we've shared, I've loved learning things about you that aren't common knowledge. To be honest, sometimes it bothers me a little to know too much, but I guess that's just part of the deal when you get to know someone so deeply. That's why I know you often doubt yourself, your abilities, and your motivations. However, I've never doubted you even once, because if anyone is capable of doing whatever they set their mind to, it's you. You're an excellent daughter, an excellent sister, an excellent friend, an excellent confidante, in fact, excellent at everything. And even if you don't value the things you do or who you are, they impress me. Sometimes you thank me for what I do or for being there. It's not that it bothers me, but I sincerely believe you have nothing to thank me for. What you receive from me isn't even a fraction of what you truly deserve, because the truth is that nothing will ever be enough for someone who gives their all. In fact, you were the one who taught me to trust myself more. I used to think that throughout life you had to sacrifice some things to have others, but you've made me want it all and feel like I can have it all. And that's because I see it in you. I see your struggle, your effort, and your unconditional support, and that inspires me to be a better version of myself every day. That incredibly unique connection we have, which goes far beyond any label, I feel exactly the same way. Perhaps in my own way and differently, but with exactly the same intensity. In your letter, you said you had decided never to leave my side. The truth is, I can't think of any logical reason for why you decided that, but I have to admit that it comforts me greatly to know that you're there and that that's the level of our loyalty. To wrap up, I know that sometimes you're afraid of not being chosen, of people being just a temporary thing. I'm sorry if this doesn't help you or if I'm not the person you want to hear this from, but I will always choose you. I've told you in the voice messages, I'm writing it to you now, and if you ask me in person, I'll tell you exactly the same thing. It's unconditional. I don't expect it to be mutual or anything like that, but, for my part, I'll do it.
